Stock chart GIF FAQ

How do I make a stock chart GIF?

Open Animate Stock on your iPhone, pick a ticker and date range, choose GIF as the export format, and tap export. You get an infinitely looping animated chart GIF saved straight to your photo library — ready to post anywhere.

Where can I post stock chart GIFs?

GIFs auto-play almost everywhere: X (Twitter), Reddit, Discord, Slack, Telegram, and email newsletters. They’re the easiest way to make a chart move in places that don’t support video embeds.

What is the quality of the exported GIF?

GIFs render at 12 fps with the exact same animation as the MP4 export, optimized for smaller file sizes that load fast in feeds. For maximum quality, export the same project as a 30 fps MP4 instead.

Can I make a GIF comparing multiple stocks?

Yes — any project type exports as a GIF: single-stock price animations, market cap growth, multi-stock comparisons, and what-if scenarios.

Do the GIFs loop?

Yes, every exported GIF loops infinitely — ideal for mesmerizing chart loops that keep people watching.
