Market cap animation FAQ

What is a market cap animation?

A market cap animation is a video that shows how a company’s total market value changed over time — for example Apple growing from a few billion dollars to over three trillion. Animate Stock renders this growth as a smooth animated chart you can export and share.

How is market cap different from stock price?

Stock price is the cost of a single share, while market cap is the total value of all shares combined. Market cap animations are great for showing company scale and growth, since they aren’t distorted by stock splits the way raw share prices are.

Can I animate the market cap of any company?

You can animate any publicly traded company with available historical data — from mega caps like Apple and Microsoft to small caps, plus major cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin.

Can I compare the market caps of two companies?

Yes. Add multiple tickers to a single project and the animation races their market caps against each other on one chart — perfect for moments where one company overtakes another.

How do I share the animation?

Export it as a high-quality video in landscape or square and save it straight to your photo library, ready to post anywhere.
